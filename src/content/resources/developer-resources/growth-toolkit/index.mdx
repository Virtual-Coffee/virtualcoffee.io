<LeadText>

Growing as a developer isn't just about writing more code. It's about building habits, showing up in community, sharing what you know, and taking care of yourself along the way. This toolkit organizes our resources into a path you can follow — pick up wherever it's most useful for where you are right now.

</LeadText>

</TextContainer>

<TextContainer>

## Build Your Foundation

The fundamentals that set you up for everything else. If you're earlier in your journey, start here. If you're more experienced, it's worth checking whether your foundation is as solid as you'd like.

- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/open-source/git-101">Git and GitHub basics</Link>** — version control, pull requests, and working with repositories
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/open-source/contributor-guide">Getting started with open source</Link>** — how to find projects, make contributions, and build your GitHub history
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/blogging">Start blogging as a developer</Link>** — write about what you're learning, build your voice, and help others
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/goal-setting">Goal setting for developers</Link>** — set a direction, break it down, and make real progress

</TextContainer>

<TextContainer>

## Grow in Community

Technical skills matter, but so does how you work with other people. These resources help you become a stronger collaborator and a more effective member of any team or community.

- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/open-source">Open source resources</Link>** — contributing, maintaining, and participating in the broader ecosystem
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/pair-programming">Pair programming</Link>** — how to pair well, what to pair on, and tools that make it easier
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/code-review">Giving and receiving feedback</Link>** — how to review code and ideas in ways that actually help
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/developer-tips/asking-coding-questions">Asking better technical questions</Link>** — how to ask for help in a way that gets you unstuck faster

</TextContainer>

<TextContainer>

## Practice the Craft

The ongoing work of getting better at building things. These are habits and practices that compound over time.

- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/open-source/maintainer-guide">Writing good documentation and READMEs</Link>** — make your work accessible to others (and future you)
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/code-review">Code review and refactoring</Link>** — how to think critically about the code you write and the code you read
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/open-source/git-101">Git workflow and commit practices</Link>** — writing meaningful commit messages and keeping a clean history

</TextContainer>

<TextContainer>

## Share Your Work

Sharing what you're learning and building is one of the highest-leverage things you can do for your career and your community. It doesn't have to be polished to be valuable.

- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/blogging">Blogging for developers</Link>** — find your topic, write your first post, and keep going
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/public-speaking">Public speaking</Link>** — from your first lightning talk to conference presentations
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/build-in-public">Learning and building in public</Link>** — how to share your progress, demos, and work-in-progress in a way that builds community

</TextContainer>

<TextContainer>

## Build Healthy Habits

Sustainable growth means taking care of yourself, not just your skills. These resources address the human side of a developer's life.

- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/developer-health/burnout">Understanding and navigating burnout</Link>** — how to recognize it, reduce it, and recover
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/developer-health">Developer health and wellbeing</Link>** — all the guides in one place
- **<Link href="/resources/developer-resources/goal-setting">Goal setting and reflection</Link>** — staying intentional about your growth without burning out in the process

</TextContainer>

## Find your next step

You don't have to follow this path in order. Use it as a map — find the section that matches where you want to grow right now, pick one resource, and start there.

If you want support along the way, the Virtual Coffee community is always here. Whether you're just starting out or you've been building for years, there's a place for you here.
